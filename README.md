## 🧠 Business Case & Initial Findings

An e-commerce company is experiencing inconsistent growth and wants to better understand customer behavior, retention, and revenue distribution.

As a data analyst, the objective of this project was to analyze transactional data to identify key drivers of business performance and uncover potential risks affecting long-term growth.

The analysis focused on:

* Evaluating core business metrics such as revenue, orders, and average order value
* Understanding customer purchasing behavior and repeat patterns
* Assessing customer engagement through purchase frequency
* Identifying how revenue is distributed across different customer segments

Initial analysis reveals two critical challenges:

* **Extremely low customer retention**, with approximately 97% of customers making only a single purchase
* **High revenue concentration**, where a small segment of customers contributes a disproportionately large share of total revenue

These findings suggest that the business is heavily dependent on continuous customer acquisition while lacking a strong base of repeat, high-value customers.

This analysis serves as a foundation for further exploration into customer experience, retention strategies, and revenue optimization in subsequent layers of the project.
